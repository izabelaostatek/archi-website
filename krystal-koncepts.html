Project Sacred Geometry Nest
Client Krystal Koncepts
Location Costa Rica
Type Sustainable Housing Concept
Year 2025
Concept

A home shaped by
geometry and nature

A concept for a sustainable residential dwelling rooted in sacred geometry principles and biophilic design. Developed for Krystal Koncepts in Costa Rica, the project explores how organic architectural forms can create spaces that feel genuinely connected to their natural surroundings.

The central living space is defined by a circular oculus — a structural skylight that draws light deep into the interior and frames the jungle canopy above. Floor-to-ceiling curved glazing wraps the perimeter, dissolving the line between inside and out. Every view is considered: the pool, the trees, the sky.

The second space demonstrates the same language in a more angular, loft-like volume — full-height glazing on the forest edge, timber ceiling, polished concrete floor. Both spaces share a material palette rooted in natural, low-impact finishes. The concept prioritises passive cooling, natural ventilation, and minimal site disruption.
